Coffee First

Coffee drinkers everywhere all share a similar problem: where can I go for good coffee, a good community and a good atmosphere? Coffee First is an app meant to remedy the problem by compiling all of the information a coffee drinker could possibly need, like locations, aesthetics, activities and more. Coffee First is your one stop to see what’s brewing!

Problem

Coffee Drinkers need a centralized hub of pertinent information for all of their coffee and community needs

Solution

Our team has created an app based solely off this need, meant to help the average coffee drinker find belonging in their vicinity.

The Process

With our app, we started from square one; we didn't have any previous app to work off of. With a lot of User research, we had a great place to start and remedied out lack of a starting point with things like a competitor analysis and a lot of mapping based on our interviews. After all of our research and Ideation, we created a clickable prototype which we ran through another round of testing. After that user testing, we iterated some more and made some better prototypes, integrating some features that our users wanted. After the research and the integration of different features, we ended up with a working, low fidelity prototype.

User Research

To begin, we iterated on a Proto Persona and actually made a couple of different personas. we did this because ideally our app would have 2 sides: a user side and a vendor side for the average coffee shop owner to present their store. We used these personas to find people to interview.
Following our interviews, we parsed through our data and synthesized some points that we could build our app off of.
Some things we discovered from our interviews that a lot of people just used google maps to find the best option for their coffee and then they typically didn’t deviate from the option they found




Post-interviews

We made several things like after our interviews like an affinity map and an empathy map, and a problem statement, a variation of which you just read at the beginning of the page. But probably the most important thing we made was our new and improved User-Persona for the average user.
We polished off our research by making a user journey map and a User flow to make sure that we really understood our users.



User Flow Map

A huge endeavor for making our new app was making a User Flow Map, which plotted out every step a user would take on our app, including what pages they would go to and what actions they could take. Below are 2 views of our map: a key/legend and a nearly full view of what our final map looked like. There were 3 previous iterations of it.

Other Tools...

Some other tools that really helped us define what we wanted for our app were the value proposition and competitor analysis we created. We mainly did the competitor analysis so we could have an easier time making an app and evaluating what's worked for our direct and indirect competition. The value proposition really helped us understand some features that our users needed from the app.

The Prototype

When me and my team worked on this prototype, we were largely new to Figma and prototyping, so we didn’t really start with a greyscale/image free version and we didn’t really gravitate towards a style tile. However some features that we integrated into our prototype were things like a basic sitemap to do things like share locations, view locations on a map and add events to a calendar.

Post-Prototype

Once we had our prototype, we devised a test for some users of our app to go through to evaluate if we needed to iterate on it further. After our final round of user testing, our main takeaways were that we needed some rewording, a skip login button, and just ultimately more buttons for navigation.

In Conclusion

While we were pretty satisfied with the functionality with our product, there were definitely more things that we could work on for a future version. In the future we could of course work on a moodboard and style tile to create a high fidelity prototype, we could also create some more interactive elements. this would something like a more interactive calendar.
